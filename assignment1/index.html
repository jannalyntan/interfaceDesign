Interaction Design Analysis

Jannalyn Tan - s4056775

Both Holdspace (Carlos Yusim) and Inter-symmetric – NNNB (Fell & Treanor) are single-page experiences, but they approach interaction very differently, one more linear and guided, the other is experimental and hands-on. This contrast is clear when comparing how they handle information design, mapping, characterisation, and feedback.

Holdspace

Holdspace is simple and clean. There’s hardly any text, and the visuals are very minimal. Using simple text to show users how to interact with the experience. It has a lot of negative space which helps focus your attention on the sound and the shape pulses along with it. Everything is stripped back so you can just take in the experience of the sound piece. Another point is the use of inverse colours, which help the user identify whether the sound is active or not. Overall, the design of Holdspace is kept minimal and clean, allowing users to fully focus on the sound rather than the design of the site.

Inter-symmetric – NNNB

On the other hand, Inter-symmetric mimics hardware synths likely to tap into users’ prior experience and encourage playful experimentation. The lack of labels supports an intuitive, discovery-driven engagement rather than step-by-step instruction. Its blue colour scheme adds some visual interest while keeping things minimal and clean. There’s no written explanation, but if you’ve seen or used a synth before, the design almost explains itself through familiarity and layout.

Holdspace

Holdspace is super straightforward. You just hold down the main button and the sound plays. The mapping is very direct, one action, one result. This keeps the interaction lightweight and keeps the user focused on the sound piece rather than controlling it. The simplicity reinforces the idea that this is something to experience, not to create with.

Inter-symmetric – NNNB

Inter-symmetric is way more open. It doesn’t tell you what to do, but that’s part of the fun. The mapping is designed to reflect real-life hardware synths, so users are encouraged to explore by clicking, dragging, or hovering over different controls. It allows for play and creation. There’s no single “correct” path, it’s all about experimenting.

In the end, both UIs succeed on their own terms, Holdspace invites quiet observation, a linear and guided experience. Having the user enjoy and listen to the sound piece. While Inter-symmetric pushes for playful interaction. It is a non-linear experience having users play and creative using the foundation sound layer of what the website already has.

Their designs reflect very different ideas about what it means to “experience” sound.
