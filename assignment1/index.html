Interaction Design Analysis

Jannalyn Tan - s4056775

Both Holdspace (Carlos Yusim) and Inter-symmetric – NNNB (Fell & Treanor) are single-page experiences, but they approach interaction very differently, one more linear and guided, the other is experimental and hands-on. This contrast is clear when comparing how they handle information design, mapping, characterisation, and feedback.

Holdspace

Holdspace is simple and clean. There’s hardly any text, and the visuals are very minimal. Using simple text to show users how to interact with the experience. Having the text fade away after users learn about the interaction. Allowing user to fully focus on the visual and sound. Everything is stripped back so you can just take in the experience of the sound piece, having the visual accompany the sound. Overall, the design of Holdspace is kept minimal and clean, allowing users to fully focus on the sound rather than the design of the site.

Inter-symmetric – NNNB

On the other hand, Inter-symmetric mimics hardware synths likely to tap into users’ prior experience and encourage playful experimentation. The lack of instructions supports an intuitive, discovery-driven engagement rather than step-by-step instruction. Even though there is a lot of elements placed on the website, there is still a structure to it, like a physical synth.

Sectioning it based on the sound, pitch and length. Having there be some label to give more information on how to use the digital synth. From on and off buttons to the different numbers on the button to educate users, giving them more information about what each buttons do. This all helping the website not give any instruction about how to interact with the website, different from Holdspace allowing users to play and explore based on familiarity and layout of a physical synth.

Holdspace

The mapping in Holdspace is straightforward and simple. Instructions appear as text; pressing and holding the main button triggers the sound. This direct mapping, one action leading to one result, keeping interactions lightweight and allows users to concentrate on the sound piece rather than the controls. The simplicity reinforces the idea that the experience is meant to be enjoyed, not manipulated.

Inter-symmetric – NNNB

On the other hand, Inter-symmetric is open-ended. It does not dictate actions, which adds to the enjoyment. The mapping mimics real-life hardware synthesizers, prompting users to explore through clicking, dragging, or hovering over various controls. Each element interacts similarly to a real synth. Users apply their prior knowledge about sliders and knobs, with labels assisting their understanding of how to interact with the site. Each interaction alters the sound in real time, promoting experimentation and creativity. There is no singular “correct” way to interact; the focus is on exploration.

Holdspace

Holdspace uses minimal elements and text. The monochromatic colour scheme and minimal geometric shapes all contribute to its view of it being more of a listening and viewing experience rather than a experimental. Having it be black and white helps to focus the viewers' attention to the sound piece instead of getting distracted. Using a geometric shape adds on to the experience making it seem more futuristic adding to the website making it seem more innovative. Showcasing this creative soundpiece that the designer has created.

It has a lot of negative space which helps focus your attention on the sound and the shape pulses along with it. Overall, the way it looks and the use of negative space supports the idea that Holdspace is not a tool or instrument, but a showcase of a sound piece. Meaning to experience it and not create with it.

Inter-symmetric – NNNB

Toggle to reveal

By contrast, Inter-symmetric characterises itself as a tool for creation. It puts the user in the role of a sound designer, inviting you to tweak, experiment, and build your own soundscape. The interface visually is more complex compared to Holdspace, filled with sliders, knobs, and buttons that mimic a physical synthesizer. This complexity gives it a sense of depth and realism. Allowing users to connect that this is a digital replicate of physical synthesiser.

Another feature that helps users understand how to interact with the website is the use of different words used, like ‘sound’, ‘pitch’, ‘length’ which gives users a hint about what the website it about. Finally, based on Plutchik colour wheel, using the blue invokes the emotion of surprise. Having users to explore and create their own sound piece. It’s a playful interface, giving the user the feeling of control, creativity, and technical engagement. While Holdspace invites viewing and admiration, Inter-symmetric invites interaction.

Holdspace uses visual feedback when loading the sound and interaction. Increasing the percentage till 100% till the screen is completely loaded up. Having this is great as it allows the sounds and visual to be fully loaded before allowing access to the site. Another feedback is the use of inverse colours, which help the user identify whether the sound is active or not. The final feedback is the use of the progress bar at the top of the screen. This bar allowed users to know how long more of the sound piece they had left, encoruaging them to finish the sound piece.

Inter-symmetric – NNNB

Inter-symmetric uses more traditional UI feedback, sliders move, dials turn, sounds shift, giving you that satisfying sense of control and response as you interact. It indictates state changes via hovers colour changes and the change of the cursor. These indicators allow users to identify whether the elements can be adjusted. As for the dials, the number change at the bottom of it informs the users that they have adjusted the dial, giving them the sense of acknowledgement that their actions were completed.

In Summary...

In the end, both UIs succeed on their own terms, Holdspace invites quiet observation, a linear and guided experience. Having the user enjoy and listen to the sound piece. While Inter-symmetric pushes for playful interaction. It is a non-linear experience having users play and creative using the foundation sound layer of what the website already has. Their designs reflect very different ideas about what it means to “experience” sound.
